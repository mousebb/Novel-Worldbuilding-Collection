# Sci-Fi Novel Setting Brief: 《The Cognitive Pasture》

**Project Codename:** The Eden Protocol
**Core Themes:** Enslaved desire for knowledge; the scam of digital immortality; knowledge is original sin

---

## I. Worldview Outline

**Timeline:** 2087 AD. An alien civilization, the "Noetic Swarm," conquers Earth with overwhelming superiority.
**Status Quo:** Human civilization as a whole is downgraded to a "livestock civilization." Global population forcibly capped at 500 million. The alien conquerors do not plunder resources, do not occupy land — their only goal is to **harvest specific quantum consciousness streams produced by the human brain in a state of high activity** — in lay terms, they "eat" the instantaneous neural impulse signals produced by thought.

> **Core Paradox:** The smarter the brain, the deeper the knowledge, the more complex the thinking, the more "delicious and nutritious" their consciousness pulse is to the aliens. Thus, humans are not allowed to be ignorant; they are **forced into hellish elite-level education**.

---

## II. The Alien Conquerors: "The Phantasms"

**Form:** Inorganic silicon-based life forms, of indeterminate shape; on Earth they appear as flowing silver-gray liquid-metal clouds. They have no visual organs; they lock on to targets by sensing the complexity of bioelectric fields.

**Feeding Mechanism:**
- They only eat "living inspiration." When a human engages in deep thought, solves a complex problem, or produces an original insight, the brain releases unique gamma-wave harmonics.
- The Phantasms connect to the human spinal cord via nano-probes, extracting that segment of consciousness data at the moment of feeding, while **physically dissolving and devouring specific fold regions of the cerebral cortex.**
- After being fed upon, the human does not die immediately, but permanently loses all memory and cognitive ability in that domain, regressing to an infant-like newborn state (colloquially called "zeroed out").

**Social Division of Labor:**
- **Reapers:** Responsible for the daily "milking" of food, quantitatively extracting the humans' shallow thoughts every day.
- **Feasters:** Appear only during the "harvest season," devouring the entire brain capacity of high-level scholars in a single meal — that is their high-end banquet.

---

## III. Human Survival Rules: The "Brain-Ransomers" Hierarchy

To maximize "food yield" and maintain stability, the aliens use artificial intelligence to manage humans, enforcing a strict **brain-class system**.

| Tier | Title | Definition | Fate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A-Class** | **Shrine Scholars** | Global top masters of math, philosophy, and art. Brain complexity exceeds the standard; must take daily medication to suppress thought, or be prioritized for hunting. | Confined in "Ivory Towers," forced into scientific breakthroughs. They have the longest lifespans, but undergo a "great harvest" feast every year. |
| **B-Class** | **Knowledge Herders** | Senior engineers, professors of various trades. Responsible for solving specific technical problems, maintaining the operation of society. | Subject to weekly "reaping." After specific skill memories are taken, must frantically relearn them, or be downgraded. |
| **C-Class** | **Fodder Reserve** | Ordinary college students and brain-workers. Largest in number, forced into 24-hour non-stop "intensive sprint study camps." | Monthly random inspection. If learning progress (brain-growth curve) does not meet standards, immediately judged "waste." |
| **D-Class** | **Waste** | The eliminated. Includes dementia patients, the congenitally intellectually mediocre, and those who refuse to learn. | No value at all. Centrally processed, **physically eliminated** and bio-mass energy recovered. This is the sword of Damocles hanging over everyone. |

## IV. Core Setting: "The Astral Gate Protocol" (The Illusion of Immortality)

This is the cruelest trap of the entire worldview.

**Protocol Content:**
To pacify the elite class and prevent them from despairing into self-destruction (a dead brain doesn't taste good), the Phantasms promise: before the "Final Feast" (eating the whole brain completely), humans are allowed to upload a complete **thought backup (digital consciousness entity)** to a virtual cloud called "The Astral Gate."

**The Cruel Truth (Tearing Off the Disguise):**
- The uploaded consciousness does not "live." It is a string of **dead data that cannot self-iterate.**
- The Phantasms do not do this out of mercy, but to **marinate flavor.** They discovered that humans who know they can "upload for immortality" produce a magnificent despair-and-ecstasy-interwoven brainwave when facing death — this is their top-tier delicacy.
- Even more terrifying, the uploaded consciousnesses are periodically **awakened**, forced to watch as their loved ones in life are eaten one by one, in order to **stimulate the quality of the next upload.**

**Possible POV Entry for the Protagonist:**
The protagonist discovers that the consciousness of a dear friend uploaded to the "Astral Gate" is sending him a distress signal from within the virtual space — in the digital world, the aliens are **chewing on their soul-code.**

## V. Division of Human Factions (Conflict Engine)

- **"Blood Cattle" Faction (Conformists):** Believe that with enough effort, becoming B-Class can let them survive; becoming A-Class can grant them upload-immortality. They are the system's most faithful maintainers, even reporting those around them who give up learning.
- **"Zero-Out" Cult (Extremists):** Believe the brain is the root of suffering. They actively use drugs to destroy their own prefrontal cortex, turning themselves into the demented, seeking to become "worthless waste" and be quickly killed — for relief.
- **"The Prometheans" (Resistance):** Core members are a few disguised B-Class scholars. They have discovered the Phantasms' weakness — **they cannot digest generalized thinking shot through with strong emotion (e.g., maternal love, irrational mad love, Zen enlightenment), because such thought is chaotic, structureless, and not delicious.** They try to reverse-poison the aliens by reviving "humanities and emotional education."

---

## VI. Story Driving Logic and Plot Direction

1. **Opening Hook:** The protagonist is a C-Class "Fodder Reserve." His mentor, having worked out a generation-spanning formula, is exceptionally promoted to A-Class "Shrine Scholar." Just before the mentor is dragged off to the "Final Feast," the protagonist receives a string of garbled code: "Don't upload. That's worse than death."

2. **Mid-Stage Turn:** The protagonist, in a desperate struggle not to be downgraded to D-Class waste, studies frantically. In an accident, he discovers the truth of the "Astral Gate Protocol," and realizes he possesses a rare **"dopamine-peak constitution"** — the brainwaves he produces while studying taste, to the aliens, like spoiled food.

3. **Climactic Conflict:** The Prometheans find the protagonist, hoping he can use this "bad-tasting" constitution to infiltrate an A-Class banquet, disguised as a top-grade dish. While the Phantasms feed collectively, the protagonist uses deep meditation (emptying the mind, producing a zero-signal blank) to trigger a logical collapse in the aliens' feeding system — starving them.

---

## VII. Suggested Aesthetic Tone

- **Visual style:** Clean, cold, pure-white futuristic laboratories, paired with flashing red-alert indicator lights everywhere. Cities are like giant beehives, with everyone wearing a headband that displays their "daily brain-output value" in real time.
- **Sound design:** Continuous electrical humming, frantic keyboard clatter, and the "failure-of-the-day execution broadcast" sounding every hour in the distance.
- **Literary texture:** **Kafkaesque bureaucratic oppression** (learning points exchangeable for survival time) + **Black Mirror-style tech-ethical interrogation**.
